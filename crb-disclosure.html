1. What is a Credit Reference Bureau (CRB)?

A Credit Reference Bureau (CRB) is a licensed institution that collects and maintains credit information on individuals and businesses. In Kenya, CRBs are regulated by the Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) under the Banking Act.

CRBs help lenders make informed lending decisions by providing credit histories of borrowers. They also help consumers build credit profiles that can improve their access to credit over time.

📊 Did You Know? Your credit report contains your credit history, including loans you've taken, repayment behavior, and any defaults. This information is used by banks, SACCOs, and other lenders to assess your creditworthiness.

3. What We Report

We report the following information about your loan to CRBs:

  • Personal Information: Name, ID number, phone number, date of birth
  • Loan Details: Loan amount, type of loan, date of disbursement
  • Repayment History: Payment dates, amounts paid, outstanding balance
  • Loan Status: Active, fully paid, or in default
  • Default Information: Amount in arrears, days overdue
  • Loan Closure: Date of final payment if loan is fully repaid

4. Positive Reporting

✅ Good News! We report positive credit information to CRBs. This means:

  • On-time payments help build your credit score
  • A good credit history increases your chances of getting future loans
  • You may qualify for lower interest rates with a strong credit profile
  • Full repayment of loans is recorded and improves your creditworthiness

Positive reporting is beneficial because it creates a credit history that demonstrates your reliability as a borrower. This can help you access larger loans with better terms in the future.

5. Negative Reporting

⚠️ Important: We are required by law to report negative credit information when you default on your loan. This includes:

  • Late payments beyond 30 days
  • Default status when payments are overdue
  • Loan write-offs or restructuring
  • Legal actions related to loan recovery

Negative information remains on your credit report for up to 5 years (or 7 years for judgments) and can significantly affect your ability to access credit from other lenders.

6. Notification Before Reporting

We are committed to fairness and transparency. Before reporting any negative information to CRBs, we will:

  • Send you a written notice via SMS and email at least 14 days before reporting
  • Provide you with details of the default and the amount owed
  • Give you an opportunity to remedy the default by making payment
  • Provide contact information to discuss repayment options

📞 Need Help? If you receive a default notice, contact us immediately at 0700 000 000. We may be able to arrange a repayment plan to prevent CRB listing.

7. How CRB Listing Affects You

Being listed negatively with a CRB can have serious consequences:

  • Difficulty getting new loans: Banks, SACCOs, and other lenders may reject your applications
  • Higher interest rates: If you qualify, you may be charged higher interest rates
  • Employment challenges: Some employers check credit reports during hiring
  • Mobile lending restrictions: Digital lenders may deny you loans
  • Long-term impact: Negative information stays on your report for up to 5 years

9. Dispute Resolution Process

If you believe the information we reported to CRBs is inaccurate, you have the right to dispute it. Follow these steps:

Step 1: Contact Imara Capital First

  • Call us at 0700 000 000
  • Email crbdisputes@imaracapital.co.ke
  • Provide your loan details and explain the inaccuracy
  • Attach any supporting documents (payment receipts, bank statements)

We will investigate and respond within 14 days. If we made an error, we will correct it and notify the CRB to update your report.

Step 2: Escalate to CRB if Unresolved

If you are not satisfied with our response, you can escalate to the CRB directly:

  • Metropol CRB: disputes@metropol.co.ke | 0709 111 000
  • TransUnion: kenya_disputes@transunion.com | 020 513 2700

Step 3: Escalate to Central Bank of Kenya

If still unresolved, you can lodge a complaint with the Central Bank of Kenya:

  • Email: ccd@centralbank.go.ke
  • Phone: 020 286 0000
  • Website: www.centralbank.go.ke

📝 Tip: Keep records of all communications, including emails, SMS, and payment receipts. These will help if you need to dispute any information.

10. Clearing Your CRB Listing

If you have been negatively listed, you can clear your record by:

  • Full Repayment: Pay the outstanding amount in full. We will update your status to "Settled" within 14 days
  • Repayment Plan: If you cannot pay in full, contact us to arrange a repayment plan. Once the plan is completed, we will update your status
  • Time: Even after settlement, negative information may remain on your report for up to 5 years, but it will be marked as "Settled" or "Closed"

✅ Once you clear your default: We will update the CRB within 7 business days to reflect your status as "Settled." You can request a free credit report to confirm the update.

11. Information Retention Period

CRBs retain information according to CBK guidelines:

  • Positive Information: Retained indefinitely (helps build your credit history)
  • Negative Information: Retained for up to 5 years
  • Judgments/Court Orders: Retained for up to 7 years
  • Fraud Alerts: Retained for the duration of the alert

12. Your Rights

Under the CRB Regulations and Data Protection Act, you have the right to:

  • Access: Request your credit report for free once per year
  • Dispute: Challenge inaccurate information at no cost
  • Correction: Have incorrect information corrected within 14 days
  • Notification: Be notified before negative information is reported
  • Consent: Provide consent before credit checks are performed
  • Compensation: Seek compensation for damages caused by inaccurate reporting
