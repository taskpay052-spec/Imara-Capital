1. Introduction

Imara Capital ("we," "our," or "us") offers you the ability to apply for loans, review documents, and sign agreements electronically. This Electronic Signature Consent ("E-Sign Consent") explains your rights and obligations when conducting business with us electronically.

📢 Important: By providing your consent, you agree to receive documents and conduct transactions electronically. This consent applies to all interactions with Imara Capital, including loan applications, agreements, disclosures, and communications.

2. Scope of Electronic Transactions

When you consent to electronic transactions, you agree that we may provide you with the following documents and communications electronically:

  • Loan applications and related forms
  • Loan agreements, promissory notes, and security documents
  • Disclosures required by law (Truth in Lending, Privacy Policy, etc.)
  • Account statements and transaction history
  • Payment reminders and due date notifications
  • Privacy notices and policy updates
  • Terms and conditions changes
  • Customer service communications
  • Credit reporting notifications
  • Collection notices and payment arrangements

We will provide these documents to you via:

  • Email to the address you provide
  • SMS text messages to your registered phone number
  • Secure links to our website or mobile platform
  • Notifications within your online account (once available)

3. Right to Withdraw Consent

You have the right to withdraw your consent to receive electronic communications at any time. However, withdrawing consent may affect your ability to obtain or maintain a loan with us, as many of our processes are designed for electronic delivery.

3.1 How to Withdraw Consent

To withdraw your consent, contact us at:

  • Email: econsent@imaracapital.co.ke
  • Phone: 0700 000 000
  • Mail: Imara Capital, Westlands, Nairobi, Kenya

3.2 Effects of Withdrawal

If you withdraw your consent:

  • We may convert your account to paper-based communications
  • We may charge a reasonable fee for paper delivery (if applicable)
  • Processing of new loan applications may be delayed
  • Existing loans will continue to be serviced, but future communications will be in paper format
  • You may be required to sign paper documents for new transactions

⚠️ Note: Withdrawal of consent does not affect the legal validity or enforceability of any documents already signed electronically.

4. Hardware & Software Requirements

To access and retain electronic communications, you will need the following:

4.1 Minimum Requirements

  • A computer, tablet, or smartphone with internet access
  • A current web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge) with 128-bit encryption enabled
  • Valid email address and phone number
  • Ability to view PDF files (Adobe Acrobat Reader or similar)
  • Sufficient storage space to save documents or a printer to print physical copies

4.2 Mobile Access

  • For mobile users, our website is optimized for smartphones and tablets
  • You may need a data connection (Wi-Fi or mobile data) to access documents
  • Documents can be saved to your device or viewed in your browser

4.3 Your Responsibility

You are responsible for maintaining the necessary hardware and software to access electronic communications. We will not be liable for any delays or failures caused by your inability to access electronic communications due to technical issues on your end.

5. Electronic Signature

By clicking "I Agree," "Sign," "Accept," or similar buttons on our website or mobile platform, you are signing documents electronically. Your electronic signature is legally binding and has the same effect as a handwritten signature under Kenyan law, including the Kenya Information and Communications Act and the Law of Contract Act.

5.1 How Electronic Signatures Work

  • When you check a box indicating agreement, you are signing electronically
  • When you click "Accept" on a loan offer, you are signing the loan agreement
  • When you type your name in a signature field, you are affirming your identity and intent
  • When you confirm via SMS or email verification, you are authenticating your signature

5.2 Intent to Sign

By using our electronic signature process, you affirm that you intend to be legally bound by the documents you sign. You acknowledge that your electronic signature is your agreement to the terms and conditions contained in those documents.

⚖️ Legal Effect: Your electronic signature has the same legal force and effect as a handwritten signature on paper. You cannot later deny the validity of an electronic signature simply because it was applied electronically.

6. Record Retention

We will retain electronic records of all documents you sign and communications we send for the period required by Kenyan law (typically 7 years from the date of loan closure). You should also retain copies of important documents for your records.

6.1 How to Save Documents

  • Download and save PDF files to your computer or device
  • Print physical copies for your records
  • Forward important emails to a secure folder
  • Take screenshots of critical information (though PDF downloads are preferred)

6.2 Accessing Historical Documents

If you need copies of previously signed documents, you may request them by contacting our support team. We may charge a reasonable fee for producing paper copies of archived documents.

7. Updating Your Information

It is your responsibility to keep your contact information current. If your email address or phone number changes, you must update it immediately by:

  • Logging into your online account (if available)
  • Contacting our customer support at 0700 000 000
  • Sending an email to updates@imaracapital.co.ke

Failure to maintain accurate contact information may result in missed communications, which could affect your loan status or lead to default.
